Are you in the right place?

We specialise in timber sash and case windows for listed and conservation area properties in Edinburgh. We use heritage slim-line double glazing on the council approved list and traditional materials including linseed putty. If your property is A-listed, B-listed, C-listed, or in a conservation area, we know exactly what is required — and we'll handle the process from survey to council sign-off.

What we do

Three ways we can help

01

Full restoration with heritage glazing upgrade

We restore your existing frames, upgrade to slim-line heritage double glazing, and add permanent draught proofing. The right solution for most listed properties.

02

Double glazing upgrade only

Where frames are structurally sound, we upgrade the glazing using high-performance gas-filled slim-line units (12mm–16mm) on Edinburgh City Council's approved list. These units are pre-approved for use in listed and conservation area properties. Prices typically range from £1,200 to £3,000 depending on window size, glazing specification, and finish. Minimal disruption. Maximum performance.

03

Like-for-like replacement

Where rot makes restoration impossible we manufacture new timber windows to match the original profile exactly. Indistinguishable from the original. Council approved.

Linseed putty is a traditional glazing compound made from linseed oil and chalk, used in timber frames for centuries. Unlike modern compounds, it remains flexible as timber moves seasonally, bonds with traditional paint systems, and is accepted by conservation officers. We use it as standard on all heritage work.
