Why it matters

The difference between a repair that lasts and one that doesn't.

Sash window repair is a skilled trade. Done properly, a repaired window can last another 30 years. Done poorly — with the wrong materials, cheap paint, or softwood beads — you'll be back within 18 months wondering why the rot has returned.

We don't cut corners. Every repair we carry out uses materials chosen for longevity, not margin. That means professional epoxy consolidants and resins (not two-part filler), the best external timber paints on the market, and Accoya glazing beads on every window we touch.

It costs a little more upfront. It's a fraction of the cost of a replacement window you shouldn't have needed.

Our standard

Three things we do differently

High-quality resin. Not two-part filler.

Two-part filler is hard, brittle, and doesn't move with timber. When the wood expands and contracts seasonally, filler cracks. Moisture gets in. The rot returns.

We use professional-grade epoxy consolidant and resin systems designed specifically for structural timber repair. They bond at a molecular level, remain slightly flexible, and last decades when applied correctly. The same systems used by heritage specialists and conservation contractors.

Best paints throughout.

Paint is the first line of defence for any timber window. The quality of the paint system — primer, undercoat, topcoat — determines how long a repair stays watertight.

We use premium external timber paints applied in full sequence. No cutting the primer. No single-coat shortcuts. The result is a finish that flexes with the timber, resists cracking, and doesn't start peeling within a year of application.

Accoya timber beads on every window.

Glazing beads are the thin strips of timber that hold the glass in the sash. They're small, but they're exposed — catching water, retaining moisture, and deteriorating faster than any other part of the window.

We fit Accoya beads as standard on every window we work on. Accoya has a 50-year above-ground durability guarantee and is used in the most demanding heritage projects in the UK. It costs more than softwood. It lasts decades longer.

Scope of work

What we repair

  • Rotten sills, rails, and stiles — cut back to sound timber and rebuilt with epoxy
  • Broken sash cords — replaced with pre-stretched waxed cord
  • Draughty sash windows — permanent brush pile draught seals fitted throughout
  • Failed paint systems — stripped, treated, primed, and repainted in full sequence
  • Broken weights and pulleys — repaired or replaced in the box frame
  • Glazing bead replacement — Accoya beads fitted as standard
  • Glazing renewal — glass replaced using heritage slim-line gas-filled units where appropriate
  • Stuck or painted-shut sashes — freed, weight-rebalanced, and returned to full operation

When repair is the right answer

If rot is localised and the structural frame is sound, repair is always preferable to replacement. It preserves original timber, avoids unnecessary waste, and costs significantly less.

We will always give you an honest assessment at survey stage. If a section is beyond economic repair, we'll tell you — and we'll quote for a like-for-like timber replacement that matches the original profile exactly.
