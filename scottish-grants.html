Scottish Grants for Listed Building Windows

A clear, honest guide to what funding is available — and what isn't — for window upgrades on A-listed and other listed properties in Scotland.

The honest picture

What grants are actually available?

There is no simple, universal grant for double glazing in listed buildings in Scotland. Most mainstream energy efficiency schemes exclude listed buildings because planning and consent restrictions limit the type of improvements that can be made. That is the reality, and anyone telling you otherwise is being misleading.

However, there are funding streams that listed building owners can explore — particularly for A-listed properties and for works that form part of a wider conservation or repair project. Below is a clear summary of what's available, who administers it, and what the conditions are.

Funding sources

Grant programmes to explore

Historic Environment Scotland — Historic Environment Repair Grant

Who it's for: Owners of A-listed properties and buildings in designated areas (conservation areas, world heritage sites).

What it covers: Repair and conservation works that preserve the character of the listed building. Window repair and heritage glazing upgrades can be eligible where they form part of an approved conservation scheme.

How much: Variable. Grants are assessed on a case-by-case basis. Projects typically require a professional conservation survey and specification.

Visit Historic Environment Scotland →

Edinburgh City Council — Conservation Area Grants

Who it's for: Owners of properties in Edinburgh's conservation areas, including New Town, Old Town, and Southside.

What it covers: Repair and restoration of traditional features — windows, doors, stonework. Eligibility depends on the specific conservation area and available funding in any given year.

How much: Typically 25–50% of approved costs, subject to means testing and annual budget availability.

Contact: Edinburgh City Council Planning & Building Standards — 0131 529 3550.

Home Energy Scotland — Warmer Homes Scotland

Who it's for: Households on lower incomes or receiving certain benefits. Not specifically for listed buildings.

What it covers: Secondary glazing may be eligible under some circumstances — it does not require listed building consent and does not alter the original windows. Standard double glazing replacements are generally not available for listed buildings.

Contact: Home Energy Scotland — 0808 808 2282 (free, impartial advice).

Energy Company Obligation (ECO4)

Who it's for: Households referred by their local council or on qualifying benefits.

What it covers: Primarily insulation and heating upgrades. Window replacement is generally excluded for listed buildings under planning restrictions. However, secondary glazing may be considered a qualifying measure in some ECO4 assessments.

Contact: Your energy supplier or Home Energy Scotland for a referral assessment.

A-Listed properties

A-Listed: where the most funding is available

A-listed buildings are Scotland's most significant historic structures — those of special architectural or historic interest. They represent around 8% of listed buildings in Scotland and attract the highest level of protection and the most funding support.

Historic Environment Scotland's grant programmes prioritise A-listed buildings. If you own an A-listed property and need to repair or upgrade your windows, a professional conservation assessment is the essential first step — it forms the basis of any grant application and establishes what works are permissible.

We have extensive experience working on A-listed properties in Edinburgh — including Craighouse and Fraser Suites Edinburgh (156 windows). We understand what conservation officers require and can advise on the glazing specifications that will be accepted.

Grants — Frequently Asked Questions

There is no universal grant for double glazing in listed buildings. Mainstream energy efficiency grants are generally not available because planning restrictions limit the type of glazing that can be installed. However, Historic Environment Scotland and Edinburgh City Council both offer funding for conservation works — including window upgrades — on eligible properties. Secondary glazing may also qualify under Home Energy Scotland schemes. Contact each body directly to check current eligibility.

Historic Environment Scotland administers grant funding for works to A-listed buildings through the Historic Environment Repair Grant programme. This can cover window repairs and heritage glazing upgrades where they form part of an approved conservation project. Applications require a professional specification and are assessed on merit. They are competitive and not guaranteed — but for A-listed properties they are the most accessible route to public funding.

Secondary glazing is worth exploring under Home Energy Scotland's Warmer Homes Scotland scheme and ECO4. Because secondary glazing does not require listed building consent and does not alter the original windows, it is treated differently from window replacement and may qualify as an energy efficiency measure. Contact Home Energy Scotland on 0808 808 2282 to check your eligibility.

Start by contacting Edinburgh City Council Planning & Building Standards (0131 529 3550) to ask about conservation area grant availability in your specific area. Then contact Historic Environment Scotland if your property is A-listed. Before applying, you'll need a professional assessment of the works required — our free survey provides this, and the written report can be used as supporting documentation for grant applications.
